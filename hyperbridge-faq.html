Hyperbridge FAQ

Who is this for? What is the ideal customer profile?
  • If you are spending meaningfully on Azure, AWS, or GCP GPU instances (P5, P5N, and similar) and want to reduce cost or improve GPU access — this is for you.
  • If you are consuming GPUs via a managed service like AWS Bedrock, Hyperbridge is not the right fit — those services abstract the GPU layer and don't expose the virtualization surface we operate on.
Is this secure?
  • The GPU is accessed over an encrypted SSL connection. The connection is outbound — no ports need to be opened on your infrastructure.
  • The GPU hardware itself is hosted at tier-1 neo-clouds with a state-of-the-art security posture.
Is there a performance penalty?

There are two effects worth knowing about:

  • Application launch time : a one-time increase of a few seconds up to a minute, as the GPU is attached and initialized.
  • Per-call latency : a few milliseconds added to each API call your application serves (for example, an inference request).

For long-running and batch applications, these are non-issues. Launch delay amortizes over the lifetime of the workload, and per-call overhead is negligible compared to actual GPU compute time.

What happens to my data?

Your data is loaded directly into GPU VRAM and never stored on any persistent media outside your VPC. Upon workload exit, the memory is wiped clean.

Your data residency requirements are obeyed since nothing persists beyond the lifecycle of your job.

What about compliance?

We are SOC 2 Type II compliant.

If you have additional compliance requirements (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP, GDPR, or sector-specific frameworks), reach out at info@cloudexe.tech and we will walk you through how Hyperbridge fits into your compliance posture.

How much work is involved in setting up a POC?

Trying Hyperbridge is straightforward. You copy our launcher binary inside your container and launch your workload command via our binary, the rest is automatic.

We've completed a full integration in as little as 15 minutes. Our team stays hands-on through your POC to make sure you hit value fast.

How do you handle workload dependencies on internal private resources?

Your containers are still running inside your VPC, only the compute is happening outside. Nothing needs to change in your ACL or networking rules. Your dependencies stay private and accessible as if the GPU is local to your infra.

How mature is this product?

Hyperbridge is currently in quasi-stealth as a standalone product, but the technology is proven. The exact same tech stack underpins our Developer Cloud product, which is actively used by many world-class universities for research workloads.

You are getting battle-tested GPU virtualization technology, now applied to the hyperscaler context. The core engine has production miles behind it.
